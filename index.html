What is this?

Your guide to the Buildathon

Four things to know before you sign up. No jargon.

01

Get the problem statements early

Problem statements are released before the main event on 25 July. Your team can start planning and building right away — no need to wait for event day.

02

Optional starter workshop for beginners

New to Kiro or coding? Join the free optional workshop on 14 August. We walk you through installing Kiro, building a simple app, and deploying it live — all in one afternoon.

03

Present, get judged, win prizes

On event day, present your solution in 4 minutes and answer 1 minute of Q&A from judges. The rest of the day is talks, networking, and the prize ceremony.

04

No coding background needed

Kiro is an AI coding assistant. If you can describe what you want to build in plain English, Kiro can help you build it. Ideas and motivation are the only prerequisites.

Rules & expectations

Team & Submission Details

Team size

Teams of 2–4 members are recommended. Solo sign-ups may be merged with other individuals at organiser discretion.

Capacity

Maximum 12 teams per problem statement. Sign up early to secure your preferred track.

Ranking preferences

In the sign-up form, rank all three problem statements by preference. Organisers match teams to their first choice where possible.

What to build

Your final project can be a simple website or prototype. A working single-file HTML app is completely valid. Focus on solving the problem well.

Early start allowed

Once problem statements are released on 25 July, your team can start building immediately. There is no restriction.

Presentation day

Prepare a working demo and a short presentation. You get 4 minutes to present and 1 minute for Q&A from the judges.

Beginner Friendly Problem Statement 1

Automated Class Recap Generator

Background

Polytechnic students frequently miss lectures or struggle to consolidate key information from dense lecture materials. Manually reviewing full slide decks is time-consuming and ineffective, especially during exam periods.

Problem

Students lack a fast and reliable way to extract the most important information from their lecture materials and assess their own understanding.

Challenge

Build a web or mobile tool that allows students to upload lecture slides or notes and receive a structured summary of key takeaways along with a short auto-generated quiz to test their understanding.

Required Tool

Teams must use Kiro to plan, build, debug, or improve their solution. AWS services such as S3, Lambda, or Amazon Bedrock may be used if they fit the idea, but they are not required.

Beginner Friendly Problem Statement 2

Peer Tutoring Platform

Background

Polytechnic students often struggle with specific modules but have no structured way to find peers who can help. Existing channels like group chats are unorganised and unreliable, making it hard to connect students who need help with those who can provide it.

Problem

There is no easy way for students to find and connect with peers who are willing and available to tutor them in specific subjects that match their schedule.

Challenge

Build a platform where students can list subjects they are able to tutor and subjects they need help with, and get matched to compatible peers based on subject overlap and availability.

Required Tool

Teams must use Kiro to plan, build, debug, or improve their solution. AWS services such as DynamoDB, Lambda, or Amazon Bedrock may be used if they fit the idea, but they are not required.

Beginner Friendly Problem Statement 3

Assignment Deadline Tracker

Background

Polytechnic students often manage multiple assignments, tests, projects, and presentations across different modules. Keeping track of important dates can be challenging, especially during busy academic periods.

Problem

Students need a simple and convenient way to keep track of upcoming deadlines and prioritise their tasks effectively.

Challenge

Build a platform that allows students to record test dates, assignment submission dates, and presentation dates. The platform should help students stay organised by providing reminders and notifications for upcoming deadlines, such as "Test in 3 days" or "Assignment due in 24 hours".

Required Tool

Teams must use Kiro to plan, build, debug, or improve their solution. AWS services such as DynamoDB, Lambda, or Amazon Bedrock may be used if they fit the idea, but they are not required.
